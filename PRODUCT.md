# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Content OS is built first for founder-creators who are building in public while also running the company. They move between product work, audience building, replies, and sales, with useful ideas and relationship signals scattered across calendars, notes, social platforms, and inboxes. They need one focused system that turns what they already know and notice into credible content and warm business conversations.

## Product Purpose

Content OS turns founder-led content and growth into one repeatable operating loop: capture signals, draft in the founder's voice, score voice quality, publish natively to LinkedIn and X, reply to relevant conversations, identify warm contacts, and feed performance back into Creator Brain.

The landing page succeeds when a qualified founder understands this complete loop within the first viewport, sees specific product evidence rather than feature claims, and starts a free trial. The central promise is direct: **Content and leads. One loop.**

## Brand Personality

Bold, tactile, kinetic, and founder-direct. Content OS feels like a crafted creative instrument with the momentum of an illustrated relay: purposeful tools, visible handoffs, strong color, and enough wit to reward exploration without turning serious work into a game. The emotional goal is creative momentum with operational confidence.

## Anti-references

Do not resemble generic AI SaaS marketing, a Linear clone, a caption generator, or a writer's portfolio. Avoid blue or purple gradient atmospheres, floating glow orbs, decorative glass-card grids, repeated feature-card sections, generic analytics dashboards, vague automation claims, dense enterprise martech language, and fabricated social proof. Do not copy Notion's illustration style, identity, layout, or components. Never use spectacle that fails to explain the product.

## Design Principles

1. **Make the loop tangible.** Show how signals become published work, replies, warm contacts, and better future content instead of describing isolated features.
2. **Let the product provide the proof.** Use the illustrated command center and realistic states such as drafts ready, Voice QA, scheduled posts, signal replies, Creator Brain learning, and warm contacts.
3. **Give each stage its own scene.** Voice, distribution, and leads use distinct kinetic poster compositions while remaining part of one relay world.
4. **Treat software as a crafted tool.** Combine precise product UI with hand-drawn characters, hard construction lines, and meaningful flat color.
5. **Reward exploration without delaying understanding.** Scroll choreography reveals handoffs, but the headline, promise, action, and product value remain immediate.
6. **Reduce deliberately on smaller screens.** Remove characters and the large dashboard where they obstruct understanding; preserve the product story through simplified diagrams.
7. **Speak with founder-level directness.** Use concise, specific copy that respects an experienced audience and avoids inflated claims.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA across the landing page. Preserve strong keyboard focus states, semantic landmarks, logical heading order, useful image alternatives, readable text measures, adequate contrast, and 44px minimum touch targets. Motion must never be required to understand the story. `prefers-reduced-motion` replaces scroll choreography, flowing connectors, cursor effects, and hover lifts with stable complete compositions.
